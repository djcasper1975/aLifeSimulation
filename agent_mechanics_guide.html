A-Life: The Evolution of Behavior

Discover the complex rules, emergent psychology, and evolving culture of your digital agents.

1. Nature vs. Nurture: Evolve or Learn?

Agents are defined by two distinct systems that shape their identity: **Genes (Nature)**, which are inherited at birth, and **Skills (Nurture)**, which are learned through action and communication.

Evolution (Genes)

Inherited traits that define an agent's potential. They only change through generations and mutation.

  • **Vision/Speed/Metabolism:** Core physical traits.
  • **Aggression:** Base chance to attack. (Only possible if **Love is 0**).
  • **Builder:** Tendency to build/repair Homes and Campfires.
  • **Mating Drive:** Energy threshold needed to seek a partner.
  • **Sociability:** How much they *need* social contact.
  • **Farming:** Tendency to plant seeds.
  • **Navigation:** Improves pathfinding efficiency and reduces energy cost for movement.
  • **Personality:** (See Section 2) Their core social archetype.

Learning (Skills)

Acquired traits that improve efficiency. These are learned by individuals and *shared* with the community via chat ('T') and the Library ('L').

  • **Building Skill:** Reduces wood cost and is gained by building/repairing.
  • **Navigation Skill:** Reduces energy cost of moving, gained by travel.
  • **Foraging Skill:** Increases energy gain from food/fruit and improves seed discovery chance.
  • **Combat Skill:** Increases damage dealt and reduces energy cost in a fight.
  • **Social Skill:** Increases social gain from chatting and is gained by social interactions.
  • **Farming Skill:** Increases the **environmental healing effect** of planting.

2. The Agent's Mind: Psychology & Society

Agents are driven by a complex psychological model that defines their well-being, social group, and capacity for conflict. A new 'Pause' state allows content agents to linger and share knowledge.

Core Needs, Sadness, and Sickness

Agents constantly balance **Energy** (from food) and **Social** (from friends/campfires).

  • **Pause Thresholds:** If Energy (>PAUSE_ENERGY_THRESHOLD) and Social (>PAUSE_SOCIAL_THRESHOLD) are high, the agent enters a contented 'Pause' state ('A' or 'o') where they passively share knowledge and seek the Library.
  • **Sad/Crisis ('s', Magenta):** If Energy or Social is below critical thresholds, the agent enters a crisis state to prioritize survival.
  • **Sickness ('Any', Green):** Agents contract Sickness if the Environmental Health is low (<ENV_SICKNESS_THRESHOLD). This incurs a heavy metabolism penalty.

Love & Apathy (The Morality Engine)

**Love** is the master stat for well-being. It is gained from positive actions (eating, socializing) and lost during 'Struggle' (low energy/social).

  • **The "Love Lock":** An agent **cannot be aggressive** if their Love score is above 0.
  • **Apathy:** If Love hits 0, the agent's compass breaks. They become **Apathetic** ('s' in magenta) for APATHY_DURATION turns, suffering a heavy metabolism penalty and social loss. This is one of the states where aggression is possible.

Personalities (The Social Fabric)

Each agent's inherited personality dictates their social interactions and conflict potential:

  • **Cooperative (1):** The standard, social agent.
  • **Isolated (2):** A-social. Prefers a SEEKING_REMOTE_SPOT ('S').
  • **Judgmental (3):** Prone to clashes with Isolated agents.
  • **Aggressive Cooperator (4):** A social agent, but highly likely to join a Vengeance posse and clashes with Judgmental agents.
  • **Clashes:** When conflicting personalities meet, they may refuse to socialize or even trigger COMBAT_CONFLICT.

3. From Survival to Culture: Building a Society

Agents build permanent structures, share knowledge, and collaborate to overcome disaster.

Communal Hubs (Homes & Campfires)

Structures grant powerful buffs but require maintenance and resource investment.

  • **Homes (H/h):** Grants major energy and social gain buffs to the owner and their children, while multiplying their metabolism cost by HOME_METABOLISM_MULTIPLIER (0.5 by default).
  • **Campfires (C):** Grants a communal social gain buff and multiplies metabolism by CAMPFIRE_METABOLISM_MULTIPLIER (0.9 by default) to nearby agents. Also provides a **passive Love gain**.
  • **Maintenance:** Homes must be repaired ('E') with wood. Campfires must be refueled ('R') with wood before their CAMPFIRE_REFUEL_THRESHOLD is reached.

Peer-to-Peer Skill Sharing

When agents **Communicate ('T')**, they share skills, knowledge, and critical news.

  • **Skill Transfer:** The agent with the higher skill transfers a fraction to the less-skilled partner.
  • **Global Contribution:** Highly skilled agents contribute to the Global Knowledge Library and their current skills determine the social gain bonus.
  • **Resource Sharing:** Agents may share carried Food/Wood/Fruit ('g') if a nearby partner is starving.
  • **Critical News:** Agents propagate news of **Food Crisis** (if world food < CRITICAL_FOOD_COUNT) and **Low Population** (if world pop < MIN_POPULATION_TARGET).

The Global Knowledge Library ('L') and Skill Focus

The Library acts as a global 'tech tree' and is boosted by the community.

  • **Passive Uptake:** All agents passively "download" knowledge from this global pool every turn, allowing new generations to be more skilled.
  • **Boosters:** Lingerers near a Campfire and Communicating agents contribute to the Library.
  • **Global Skill Focus:** A special setting allows a chosen skill (e.g., 'farming') to receive a small, continuous boost (<GLOBAL_SKILL_BOOST) across all agents, shaping the direction of the culture.

4. The Dark Side: Conflict, Crime & Vengeance

Violence emerges from desperation, personality, and tragedy. Combat involves a reduction to the agent's **Combat Skill** and a deduction of the ATTACK_COST_BASE energy.

Aggression & Retaliation ('X', 'r')

  • **Trigger:** An agent can initiate an attack ('X') if their **Love is 0** (Apathetic) and they roll a low enough chance against their **Aggression gene**.
  • **Conflict Attack:** Clashing personalities (e.g., Judgmental and Isolated) may force an attack.
  • **Retaliation:** The victim will **immediately** stop all other tasks to hunt down their attacker ('r').

The Vengeance System ('V')

A system of vigilante justice triggered by child mortality.

  • **Trigger:** If a **child (age < ADULT_AGE) dies**, nearby agent-witnesses will blame the child's closest living parent and enter the **Avenging ('V')** state for VENGEANCE_DURATION turns.
  • **The Posse:** An avenging agent actively attempts to **recruit other nearby agents** into a "posse" to hunt down the "negligent" parent.
  • **Consequence:** A successful hunt results in a COMBAT_VENDETTA death.

5. A Living, Breathing World

The world has a key attribute—Environmental Health—which provides negative feedback to over-harvesting populations.

Environmental Impact & Sickness

The world's **Environmental Health** score is influenced by all agent actions.

  • **Degradation:** Gathering food/wood, running active campfires (Pollution: <ENV_DECAY_CAMPFIRE_POLLUTION), and high local agent density (Overpopulation: <ENV_OVERPOPULATION_DECAY) all damage the environment.
  • **Healing:** Planting seeds ('p') *heals* the environment, scaled by the agent's **Farming Skill**.
  • **Sickness Feedback:** If Environmental Health drops below a critical threshold (<ENV_SICKNESS_THRESHOLD), agents will have a chance to contract **Sickness** ('s' in green), leading to high metabolism penalties and death by STARVATION_SICKNESS.

Resource Specialization (The Fruit System)

Three different types of fruit spawn, offering specialized, tactical buffs. Agents prioritize fruit seeking based on their needs.

  • **Energy Fruit ('R'):** Standard high-energy food.
  • **Social Fruit ('P'):** Provides a large boost to the agent's Social need. Agents may seek this if Social is low.
  • **Speed Fruit ('B'):** Provides a powerful movement speed buff for a short duration. Agents may seek this opportunistically.
  • **Planting Mandate:** Agents carry Food Seeds, Wood Seeds, and Fruit Seeds. They are **mandated** to plant them during critical global crises (low food/pop).

6. An Agent's "AI": The Priority List

An agent's decision is based on the first condition met in a strict priority list. This hierarchy defines their behavior and state ('A', 'f', 'b', etc.).

  1. Conflict / Retaliation:

    2. Retaliation ('r') or Avenging ('V') a child's death.

  2. Crisis State (Apathy/Sad):

    3. If Energy/Social is critical, enter 'Sad' ('s') to find help/resources/library.

  3. Global Crisis Response:

    4. If **Food Crisis News** is active, **must** plant seeds ('p'). If nearby agent is starving, **must** share resources ('g'). If **Low Pop News** is active, seek mate ('M').

  4. Energy & Survival:

    5. If Energy is low, eat carried food/fruit, or find food/fruit ('f').

  5. Campfire Refueling:

    6. If a nearby Campfire is dying, get wood ('w') or refuel it ('R').

  6. Home Repair:

    7. If Home is damaged and agent is owner, get wood ('w') or repair it ('E').

  7. Social Needs:

    8. If Social is low, seek happy agents, Campfires, or the Library ('t'/'L').

  8. Home Building/Claiming:

    9. If homeless, claim empty Home ('k') or build new one ('b').

  9. Farming (Normal):

    10. If all needs are met and they have seeds, plant them ('p').

  10. Wandering & Contentment:

    11. If all needs are met, wander ('A') and explore. Agents may 'Pause' to share knowledge if content.

7. Settings Expert Guide: Shaping the World

The 'Settings' panel allows you to modify the core constants that govern evolution, behavior, and survival. Use these to become an expert simulation designer!

Simulation & World Parameters

SIM_SPEED (ms): The time interval between turns. Lower values speed up the simulation.

STARTING_AGENTS/FOOD/WOOD: Initial population and resource counts. High starting food/wood encourages immediate building/socializing.

WORLD_WIDTH/HEIGHT: Size of the map. Larger maps necessitate stronger Navigation genes/skills.

MIN_POPULATION_TARGET: The threshold for **Low Population News**. Below this, agents prioritize mating/sharing news.

CRITICAL_FOOD_COUNT: The threshold for **Food Crisis News**. Below this, agents prioritize planting seeds.

GLOBAL_SKILL_FOCUS: A small, continuous learning boost (<GLOBAL_SKILL_BOOST) applied to this skill for all agents. Useful for forcing a cultural direction (e.g., 'farming').

Resources, Farming & Fruit

FOOD/WOOD_SPAWN_RATE: How often new resources spawn. Controlled by `Environmental Health`.

FOOD_FRESHNESS: How long food lasts before spoiling.

GROW_TIME (Food/Tree/Fruit): Turns required for a planted seed to yield a resource. Lower values favor Farming agents.

WOOD/FOOD/FRUIT_SEED_CHANCE: Base chance to find a seed when gathering/eating. This is boosted by **Foraging Skill**.

FRUIT_BENEFIT_ENERGY_VAL: Energy gain from an Energy Fruit ('R').

FRUIT_BENEFIT_SOCIAL_SOCIAL_VAL: Social gain from a Social Fruit ('P').

FRUIT_BENEFIT_SPEED_DURATION: The length of the speed buff from a Speed Fruit ('B').

Structures, Age & Social

CAMPFIRE_BURN_TIME/WOOD_COST: The total lifespan and initial cost of a Campfire.

CAMPFIRE_REFUEL_THRESHOLD: The timer threshold below which agents will prioritize refueling the fire ('R').

HOME_DECAY_RATE: How often a Home loses 1 durability. Higher numbers mean slower decay.

ADULT/OLD/MAX_AGE: Defines the agent's life stages.

PAUSE_ENERGY/SOCIAL_THRESHOLD: The minimum required Energy/Social for an agent to enter the content 'Pause' state.

STARTING_LOVE / LOVE_GAIN: Base Love score and how much is gained from positive actions (Eat, Social, Rest). Low Love enables aggression.

APATHY/VENGEANCE_DURATION: How long agents remain in the Apathy ('s') or Avenging ('V') state.

Developer Overrides (Core Physics)

These settings control the most fundamental constants and are best left alone unless you want to radically alter the 'physics' of the simulation.

MOVE_BASE_ENERGY_COST: The base energy drain per movement step.

NAV_MIN_COST_MULTIPLIER: The lowest possible energy cost for movement, even with max Navigation Skill.

CHILD_METABOLISM_CAP: The maximum metabolism cost a non-adult agent can incur, preventing child agents from immediately starving due to high parental costs.

MATE_ENERGY_COST: The energy penalty for mating.

GENE_..._MIN/MAX/MUTATE_RATE: Hard constraints for the evolution process. These directly control the possible range and mutation volatility of inherited genes.
