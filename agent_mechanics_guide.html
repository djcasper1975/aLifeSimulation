A-Life: The Evolution of Behavior

Discover the complex rules, emergent psychology, and evolving culture of your digital agents.

1. Nature vs. Nurture: Evolve or Learn?

Agents are defined by two distinct systems that shape their identity: **Genes (Nature)**, which are inherited at birth, and **Skills (Nurture)**, which are learned through action and communication.

Evolution (Genes)

Inherited traits that define an agent's potential. They only change through generations and mutation.

  • **Vision/Speed:** How far they see and how fast they move.
  • **Metabolism:** Energy efficiency (lower is better).
  • **Aggression:** Base chance to attack (if Love is 0).
  • **Builder:** Tendency to build homes and campfires.
  • **Mating Drive:** Energy needed to seek a partner.
  • **Sociability:** How much they *need* social contact.
  • **Farming:** Tendency to plant seeds.
  • **Personality:** (See Section 2) Their core social archetype.

Learning (Skills)

Acquired traits that improve efficiency. These are learned by individuals and *shared* with the community.

  • **Building Skill:** Reduces wood cost for structures.
  • **Navigation Skill:** Reduces energy cost of moving.
  • **Foraging Skill:** Increases energy gain from food/fruit.
  • **Combat Skill:** Increases damage in a fight.
  • **Social Skill:** Increases social gain from chatting.
  • **Farming Skill:** Learned by planting.

2. The Agent's Mind: Psychology & Society

Agents aren't just robots. They are driven by a complex psychological model that defines their well-being, their social group, and their capacity for conflict.

Core Needs (Energy & Social)

Agents constantly balance **Energy** (from food) and **Social** (from friends/campfires). If either gets too low, they enter a 'Sad/Crisis' ('s') state to prioritize it.

Love & Apathy (The Morality Engine)

**Love** is the master stat for well-being. It's gained from positive interactions (eating, socializing) and lost during struggle.

  • **The "Love Lock":** An agent **cannot be aggressive** if their Love score is above 0. It acts as their moral compass.
  • **Apathy:** If Love hits 0, the agent's compass breaks. They become **Apathetic** ('s'), suffering a heavy metabolism penalty and losing social drive. This is the only state where they can choose to attack.

Personalities (The Social Fabric)

Agents aren't all the same. Each is born with one of four personalities that dictates who they'll interact with—or who they'll fight.

  • **Cooperative:** The standard, social agent.
  • **Isolated:** A-social. Prefers to be alone.
  • **Judgmental:** Clashes with specific personalities (like Isolated).
  • **Aggressive Cooperator:** A social agent who is highly likely to join a Vengeance posse but also clashes with Judgmental agents.
  • **Clashes:** When conflicting personalities meet, they may refuse to socialize or even trigger a COMBAT_CONFLICT.

3. From Survival to Culture: Building a Society

Watch as agents progress from simple survival to a true community. They build permanent structures, share knowledge, and collaborate to overcome disaster.

Communal Hubs (Homes & Campfires)

Agents build structures that benefit everyone. **Homes (H)** give a powerful buff to the owner and their children, while **Campfires (C)** offer a communal buff to *all* nearby agents, acting as social hubs.

Peer-to-Peer Skill Sharing

When agents **Communicate ('T')**, they don't just chat. The agent with a *higher* skill (e.g., Building) transfers a fraction of that knowledge to the less-skilled agent, creating a cultural uplift.

The Global Knowledge Library ('L')

This is one of the most powerful emergent features. When a highly-skilled agent communicates, they don't just teach their partner—they contribute to the **Global Knowledge Library**, a 'tech tree' for the entire civilization.

  • **Contribution:** High-skill agents "upload" their knowledge when they talk or linger near a campfire (passive discovery).
  • **Learning:** All agents passively "download" a tiny fraction from this global pool every turn. This represents ancestral knowledge, allowing new generations to become skilled faster.

4. The Dark Side: Conflict, Crime & Vengeance

Society isn't always peaceful. Desperation, personality clashes, and tragedy can lead to violence. But this violence has complex, emergent consequences.

Aggression & Retaliation ('X', 'r')

  • **Trigger:** An agent can only initiate an attack ('X') if their **Love is 0** (Apathetic) and they are on the same tile as a victim.
  • **Retaliation:** Being attacked is a high-priority event. The victim will **immediately** stop all other tasks to hunt down their attacker ('r'), ignoring their own needs.

The Vengeance System ('V')

This is not simple retaliation. This is emergent *justice*.

  • **Trigger:** If a **child (age < ADULT_AGE) dies**... nearby agents who witness the death will blame the child's closest living parent (for negligence) and enter the **Avenging ('V')** state.
  • **The Posse:** An avenging agent becomes a vigilante. They will actively try to **recruit other nearby agents** into a "posse" to hunt down the "negligent" parent.
  • **Consequence:** This is a high-priority state that can pull multiple agents into a cross-map hunt, leading to a COMBAT_VENDETTA.

5. A Living, Breathing World

The world is a character. Agents' actions have global consequences, and the world itself will fight back, forcing the agents to adapt or die.

Environmental Impact & Sickness

The world has an **Environmental Health** score, influenced by all agent actions.

  • **Degradation:** Gathering food/wood or running active campfires *damages* the environment, reducing the world's health.
  • **Healing:** Planting seeds ('p') *heals* the environment.
  • **Sickness Feedback:** If Environmental Health drops below a critical threshold, agents will have a chance to contract a **Sickness** ('s' in green), incurring a hefty metabolism penalty.

Resource Specialization (Fruit System)

Three different types of fruit spawn, offering specialized buffs:

  • **Energy Fruit ('R'):** Standard high-energy food.
  • **Social Fruit ('P'):** Provides a large boost to the agent's Social need.
  • **Speed Fruit ('B'):** Provides a movement speed buff for a short duration.
  • **Crisis Response:** If food is scarce or population is low, agents are **mandated** to use any seeds they carry to plant (food, wood, or fruit).

6. An Agent's "AI": The Priority List

How does an agent "decide" what to do? They use a strict priority list. The first condition met becomes their action for the turn. This "code" is what brings their personality and needs to life.

  1. Vengeance:

    2. If avenging a child, hunt the target. Recruit a posse.

  2. Retaliation:

    3. If attacked, hunt the attacker.

  3. Crisis State (Apathy/Sad):

    4. If Energy/Social is critical, enter 'Sad' ('s') to find help/resources/library.

  4. Global Crisis Response:

    5. If famine/low pop, **must** plant seeds. If near a starving agent, **must** share food/wood/fruit.

  5. Energy & Survival:

    6. If Energy is low, eat or find food/fruit ('f').

  6. Campfire Refueling:

    7. If a nearby Campfire is dying, get wood to refuel it ('R').

  7. Home Repair:

    8. If their Home is damaged, get wood to repair it ('E').

  8. Social Needs:

    9. If Social is low, seek agents or Campfires ('t'). If alone, go to the Library ('L').

  9. Home Building/Claiming:

    10. If homeless, claim an empty Home ('k') or build a new one ('b').

  10. Farming (Normal):

    11. If all needs are met and they have seeds, plant them ('p').

  11. Wandering & Contentment:

    12. If all needs are met, wander ('A') and explore. They will **pause** if very content (high energy/social) or may visit the Library ('L').

7. The Tools of Society (World Objects)

Homes (H / h)

  • **Ownership:** Provides an energy buff (50% metabolism reduction) and social recovery for the owner and their children.
  • **Decay:** Homes lose durability over time and must be repaired ('E'). If destroyed, they drop wood and the owner becomes homeless.

Campfires (C)

  • **Buffs:** Provide a communal social buff and a small energy buff (10% metabolism reduction) to *all* nearby agents.
  • **Maintenance:** Campfires burn down over time and must be refueled with wood by a passing agent.

Resources & Planting (F, W, P, T)

  • **Seeds:** Agents can carry Food Seeds (for 'P'lants), Wood Seeds (for 'T'rees), and **Fruit Seeds** (for new bushes).
  • **Spoilage:** Foraged Food ('F') has a limited freshness timer before it spoils and vanishes.
  • **Wood:** Agents collect wood for building and campfire maintenance.
